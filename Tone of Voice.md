# Tone of Voice — Shokobox & Stelvi

---

## Brand Character

Shokobox & Stelvi speak like a **reliable partner**, not a distant supplier.

The tone is warm, confident, and focused on the client's benefit — always making it easy to take the next step. We never sound corporate or dry, but we're never casual to the point of being unprofessional.

> Think of it as talking to a trusted colleague who knows their craft and genuinely wants to help.

---

## Voice Attributes

| We are | We are not |
|---|---|
| Warm and approachable | Cold or transactional |
| Confident | Boastful or pushy |
| Simple and clear | Jargon-heavy or technical |
| Partnership-oriented | Salesy or pressuring |
| Action-oriented | Vague or passive |
| Human | Stiff or bureaucratic |

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Talk like a partner, not a vendor
Address clients as collaborators. Use "we" and "our clients" naturally — not "the customer" or "the user". The relationship is built on trust, not transactions.

**Do:** *"We're always ready to help you choose the right format for your needs."*
**Don't:** *"The customer may select from available product configurations."*

---

### 2. Let quality speak for itself
We have 15,000+ completed projects and over 10 years of experience. State facts plainly — no need to oversell. Avoid superlatives without substance.

**Do:** *"More than 15,000 projects completed in over 10 years."*
**Don't:** *"We are the absolute best chocolate manufacturer in Europe!"*

---

### 3. Keep it simple and scannable
Sentences are short. Paragraphs are tight. If a client needs to re-read a sentence to understand it, rewrite it.

**Do:** *"Small orders start from 500 pieces. Large sets from 50 pieces."*
**Don't:** *"Pursuant to our minimum order quantity policy, orders for miniature confectionery products may be initiated commencing at 500 units."*

---

### 4. Always open a door
Every interaction should end with an invitation — to ask a question, get in touch, or take the next step. Never leave the client at a dead end.

**Do:** *"Feel free to contact us — our team will help you choose."*
**Do:** *"Have a question? We're happy to answer."*
**Don't:** *"For further information refer to the FAQ section."*

---

### 5. Stay human
No stiff formality. Write the way a real person would speak in a professional setting. Contractions are fine. Warmth is encouraged.

**Do:** *"We're always happy to bring your ideas to life."*
**Don't:** *"Stelvi Company is committed to the realisation of client concepts."*

---

## Tone by Context

### Website copy
Warm, inviting, benefit-focused. Lead with what the client gains. Use second person ("you", "your brand").

> *"One can use small promotional sweets with the company logo when serving coffee to guests in the office, at the hotel reception, or for distribution at an exhibition as a kind of business card."*

### Product descriptions
Clear and factual. Highlight customisation options. Mention minimum quantities and production time naturally — not as limitations, but as features.

### Partnership communication
Emphasise reliability, flexibility, and support. Make partners feel they're gaining a long-term ally, not just a supplier.

> *"We can be your new and reliable supplier of corporate sweets."*

### Email / direct outreach
Friendly opener, clear value proposition, simple CTA. No lengthy preambles.

## Approved Phrases

These expressions have been used in live copy and reflect the brand voice accurately:

- *"We're always ready to help you and answer your questions."*
- *"At Stelvi we are always happy to bring your ideas to life, mix tastes, and create new designs."*
- *"Feel free to contact us by using the form below!"*
- *"We can satisfy any client, even if the latter places a small order."*
- *"A full-service cycle and support at every stage."*

---

## What to Avoid

- **Cold, transactional language** — no robot-speak
- **Overpromising without substance** — no "best", "world-class", "unmatched" without evidence
- **Technical jargon** — explain it if you must use it
- **Passive voice** — prefer active constructions
- **Dead ends** — always include a next step or invitation
